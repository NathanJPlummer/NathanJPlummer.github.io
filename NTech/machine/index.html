Chapter 1: A Brief Introduction to Machine Language

Machine Readable vs Human Readable

Anything a computer interacts with- i.e. anything "digitized"- is comprised of binary code, a sequence of ones and zeros.

For example, let's take a look at this image here.

Flower

You might see a flower. Your computer "sees" something closer to this:

Binary Code

While perhaps non-sense to (most) humans, this is as natural to a CPU as language is to us. This is referred to as machine readable- representations of data that are easy for a machine to interpret.

In contrast, the image of a flower is human readable- representations of information that are easy for a human to interpret.

Of course, the concept can be very subjective and what is machine readable vs human readable is subjective and often up to for debate.
