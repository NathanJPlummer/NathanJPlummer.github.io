FADE IN: A COMPLETELY BLACK SCREEN.  THE FOLLOWING QUOTE IS
SUPERED:
          "Aristotle believed that during
          sleep your soul roams free. What it
          sees are dreams. Skilled dreamers
          control what they see. There is a
          theory that there are two gates
          your soul can enter, one a positive
          gate, the other a negative. The
          Dream Master guards the positive
          gate and protects its sleeping
          host.
          There are fewer theories about the
          negative gate..."
 
INT. JESSICA'S CAR - DAY
 
The inside of a beaten up Saturn, engine sputtering in a
desperate cling to life.  Bits of trash and a hastily
unfolded MAP of SPRINGWOOD litter the back seat.
 
In the driver's seat- JESSICA.  A particularly pretty
girl-next-door type.  Her at first plain appearance- black
tight t-shirt, faded jeans, pink sneakers- are enhanced by
just the hint of a wild streak; a studded metal belt
accompanies a literal blue streak down her long black hair.
 
She displays obvious signs of exhaustion- dark circles,
irregular breathing.  She sighs and yawns her way through a-
 
Jessica hits the breaks with a sudden force, causing her
tires to SCREEECH in the process!  Her head bounces back and
forth from the momentum. 
 
-Stop sign.
 
                      JESSICA
          Damnit.
 
Jessica continues to drive.
 
Her head nods...
 
Her eyelids close... 
 
Her PHONE rings, jolting her awake!
 
Her boyfriend, Tony, is on speakerphone.
 
                      JESSICA
          Baby, thank god.  The stupid GPS
          keeps sending me in circles.
 
                      TONY
          I've got a map right here.  It's
          easy.
 
Briefly Jessica glances over at the map in her back seat,
and rubs her eyes.  This is not comforting.
 
A BEEP.
 
                      JESSICA
          Hold on, low battery.  Maybe I can
          find a phone to-
 
The PHONE DIES.
 
                      JESSICA
          Yeah.  Okay.
 
Jessica pulls over. 
 
EXT. FREDDY'S HOUSE - DAY
 
Jessica exits her car. 
 
A suburban neighborhood.  One imagines children playing tag
in the yard, a dog chasing a ball down the street, and folks
in aprons grilling burgers.
 
Yet there are none of these things.  Nothing except...
emptiness.  Not a car, bicycle, or even passing vehicle in
sight.
 
It's quiet.  Alarmingly quiet, the only vestige of
civilization a piece of PAPER floating down a nearby
sidewalk.  Gliding effortlessly through the wind...
 
...except there is no wind.  There is not even the 
sound
 of wind.
 
Jessica grabs the paper.
 
A chalk drawing, detailed yet crude, as if drawn by a young
child.  The HOUSE in front of Jessica.  Instinctively she
folds the drawing up and places it in her pocket.
 
Down the road a young man jogs, wearing over sized green
headphones and red jogging shorts.
 
Despite wearing sneakers on a paved sidewalk his feet make
no sound. 
 
The young man is thin, but well built.  A Dawson's Creek era
Kerr Smith type with an inquisitive look plastered on to his
face.  He moves with an almost effortless grace, but his
eyes are nearly glazed, looking ahead but looking 
through
 everything.
 
Directionless.
 
This young man is JACK, and he's a close friend of
Jessica's.  Their gazes meet, and both show a sense of
confusion- but curiously not surprise- that they have both
found themselves in this picket fence wastleland.
 
Upon reaching Jessica, Jack removes his headphones from his
ears and places them on his neck.  He pulls out a Samsung
Galaxy SMARTPHONE.  He continues to jog in place.
 
                      JACK
          What are you doing here?
 
                      JESSICA
          Lost.  Where are we?
 
                      JACK
          No idea.
 
Without ceremony Jack jogs away from Jessica.
 
                      JESSICA
          Wait, do you have a phone!?
 
                      JACK
          Gotta keep my heart rate up!  Try
          that house in front of you.  No one
          lives there!
 
Jessica frowns.  Her movement shows hints of natural
leadership; observant.  She approaches the HOUSE with
caution, but not fear. 
 
She 
senses
, more than she 
knows
, something is off.
 
INT. FREDDY'S HOUSE- ENTRANCE HALLWAY - DAY
 
An ALARM CLOCK- 3:33 PM.  The time changes to 3:32 PM.
 
The house is well lit, but otherwise worn.  Curiously the
lights are on, but there are signs the place has been long
abandoned.
 
An empty round wooden table is surround by four matching
chairs, each in various stages of decay.  The one on the far
right is missing a leg- laying splintered beside it- and is
lopsided.  A chandelier with eight small light bulbs- three
of which are burnt out- sways back and forth.
 
A fourth light bulb sputters before blacking out.
 
The front door CREAKS open, slowly.  In front; Jessica,
standing idle, almost lifeless.  Her hand not on the
doorknob- she didn't open the door so much as it opened 
for
 her.  Welcoming her.  Her CAR remains parked across the
street.
 
She takes a step forward- second guesses herself.  Then
takes a step back.  She looks more confused then worried-
some inaccessible area of her mind knows this is unusual,
but is either unwilling or 
unable
 to register fear.
 
She takes a quick glance at her phone- still dead- places it
back into her back pocket... and steps inside.
 
                      JESSICA
          Hello?
 
Jessica places her PURSE on the wooden table and walks into
the kitchen.
 
INT. FREDDY'S KITCHEN - CONTINUOUS
 
The kitchen is similarly abandoned.  Overly bright with a
1950s retro-style lamp hanging from the ceiling.  Various
dishes litter a quarter full sink, inhabited by a single
fly- its obnoxious buzzing the only faint noise in the
house.  To the back right a GREEN AND RED DOOR- out of place
with the aesthetic of the rest of the house.
 
Jessica visualy suveys the room.  No phone, just an empty
PHONE JACK on the wall.  Time to move on.
 
Jessica approaches the GREEN AND RED DOOR; puts her hand on
the handle.  She turns it slightly; stops. 
 
A PHONE CORD sticks out of a cabinet drawer next to the
sink.  Noticing it, Jessica wonders...could it be?  Jessica
opens it- a PHONE! 
 
Jessica sticks the phone's cord into the jack, attempting to
dial out.
 
Nope; dead.  Time to move on.
 
Jessica RETURNS THE PHONE to the drawer.
 
She checks the time on her cell phone.  6:27 PM (NOTE:
intentional continuity errors).
 
Jessica pauses; her cautious look returns- something's
askew. 
 
She snaps out of it, sighs, and exits the house.
 
INT. FREDDY'S HOUSE- ENTRANCE HALLWAY- CONTINUOUS
 
Jessica exits the front door, closing it behind her.  She
does not notice her CAR is no longer parked in front.  Her
PURSE remains on top of the dinning table
 
A stillness overtakes the house.  What once seemed ominous
and filled with macabre potential is now lifeless; void.
 
An unusually long pause- then:
 
The ALARM CLOCK:  Time- 7:42. 
 
The kitchen PHONE RINGS four times; stops.
 
Another unusually long pause.  The unexpected calm returns
to the house.  Perhaps, Jessica truly is safe.  Perhaps, she
truly will-
 
-Jessica barges in and grabs her purse!
 
CLOSE-UP on the front door, which SLAMS shut.
 
The BULBS from the hanging chandelier all BURST save one. 
 
REVEAL- it's suddenly NIGHT time.
 
Not only is it dark outisde, but the INTERIOR OF THE HOUSE
HAS CHANGED- old and decrepit.  Run down and dirty.  The
chairs aren't just in decay, they're full on rotting. 
Maggots and centipedes slither through the wooden crevices. 
On the the table are four long adjacent MARKS, bringing
attention to a nearby BLOOD STAIN.
 
Sound: KNIVES RUBBING AGAINST EACH OTHER.
 
FREDDY Krueger- burnt flesh, menacing sneer, dirty red and
green sweater- stands at the front door, inches from
Jessica.
 
Jessica runs towards the kitchen, tripping over a chair,
swatting centipedes off her legs as she runs. 
 
Freddy follows.
 
INT. FREDDY'S KITCHEN - CONTINUOUS
 
Jessica grabs the doorknob to the red/green door.  It closes
itself. 
 
Jessica pulls again, harder, bursting it wide open
 
INT. BOILER ROOM - CONTINUOUS
 
If the upstairs was dirty, the boiler room is all out
disgusting.  A single orange bulb hanging loose from the
ceiling is the only source of light.  Everything single inch
is covered with grime and dust.  There are enough blood
stains that no particular one stands out- just decoration.
 
Jessica tepidly walks down a stairway.  The wooden boards it
was built with have long since been compromised, and each
one makes a hideous, splintering crunch as she walks on it.
 
SQUREECH!  METAL SCRAPES METAL. 
 
From the opposite side of the room the sound repeats.  THE
SOUND REPEATS in different directions, picking up rhythm.
Jessica can't help but close her eyes and cover her ears. 
 
Jessica's POV.  A BLACK SCREEN AND UNCOMFORTABLE SQUREECHING
NOISES.
 
The noises stop.  End POV.
 
Jessica walks further down the stairs.  One stair starts to
crackle- the wood is too rotten and about to collapse under
her weight.  Jessica tepidly looks down and-
 
Through the stairway gap Freddy's GLOVE grabs Jessica's
ANKLE, tripping her. 
 
Jessica TUMBLES down the stairs.
 
She picks herself up, running further into the boiler room,
next to a furnace. The furnace LIGHTS UP, flames engulfing
the inside.  Jessica instinctively jumps back.
 
Freddy, a previously invisible force, appears on her right
side. 
 
He SWIPES- cuts on her right arm, and rips in her shirt. 
She'll live.
 
Jessica runs through a hallway, and various HANDS come out
of the wall, attempting to grab her, accompanied by
tormented screams.  Freddys lost souls.
 
Jessica continues to run until she reaches an IVORY DOOR.
 
Jessica hesitates.  Something's off.  This door doesn't fit
with this world.  It doesn't belong here.
 
From behind her Freddy appears, glove raised, ready to
strike...
 
The door opens.  A set of WOMAN'S ARMS pulls Jessica
through.
 
INT. ADULT FREDDY'S APARTMENT  LIVING ROOM - DAY - 1960S
 
An apartment in the 1960s.  Almost completely empty, save
for a cheap, worn bed in the corner.  No sheets. 
 
The only defining characteristic is a large hole in the
right side wall, cracks splintering out beneath it. 
Underneath the hole, on the floor, is a flipped UPSIDE DOWN
CRUCIFIX. 
 
Jessica finds herself in the middle of the apartment;
disoriented.  Visible and in front of her is a BATHROOM.
 
Inside the bathroom:  A PRIEST wearing his ROBERT, paces
back and forth, his movements manic yet with purpose.  He
keeps grabbing various items and throwing them in the
bathrub.  Jessica cautiously approaches him.
 
FREDDY'S APARTMENT BATHROOM - DAY - 1960S
 
The bathtub is filled with religious paraphernalia. 
Crosses, a chalice, a bible, etc. There's a canister of
GASOLINE in the sink.
 
The man is an ADULT FREDDY Krueger.  Not the monster we
know, but a human.  His eyes are bloodshot and raw, the
result of a mixture of lack of sleep and crying.  His hands
furiously grab items, throwing them in with little cerimony
or regard.  He either ignores or is unaware of Jessica's
presence.
 
Satisified he's arranged everything properly, he POURS
gasoline into the tub.
 
With a sudden sense of deliberateness and awareness he turns
around and looks Jessica straight in the eyes.  His face is
a twisted mess of rage, his breathing heavy and spiteful. 
Instinctively Jessica steps back.
 
                      ADULT FREDDY
          This is where it starts.
 
He removes his robe, nothing but underwear beneath.  He
throws the robe in the bathtub.
 
He looks at Jessica once more, and this time he looks calm. 
Same determinism, but now there's a sense of finality.  Of
peace.
 
He lights a MATCH.
 
                      JESSICA
          Wait!
 
INT. WESTIN HILLS - HALLWAY - NIGHT
 
Jessica opens her eyes.
