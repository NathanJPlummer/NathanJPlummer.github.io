Examining Disinfection by-Products During the Production of High Quality Recycled Water

Student Researcher: Ariel Atkinson, BS

Objectives: The primary objective of this project is to investigate the regulated and emerging non-regulated disinfection byproducts (DBPs) that can be generated during the production of High Quality Recycled Water (HQRW) at advanced wastewater treatment plants (AWTPs) in South East Queensland, Australia. This will be primarily achieved at those plants using reverse osmosis (RO) membranes. This objective will be met by the following specific project goals:

  1. Gain an in-depth understanding of the occurrence, fate and mechanisms of DBP formation across the different barriers of the HQRW production treatment train when different disinfectants are used (chloramines, advanced oxidation processes, and final chlorination) as well as the impact of operational conditions.
  2. Understand the mechanism of removal of DBPs and their precursors by RO and nanofiltration (NF) membranes under different operational conditions, including fouling events.
  3. Propose strategies to control and minimize the formation of specific DBPs of concern within an AWTP
